Reducing traffic stop escalation through real-world video training

SAFE STOP CALIFORNIA is designed to educate drivers on lawful behavior during traffic stops, reduce unnecessary escalation, and improve safety outcomes for both the public and law enforcement.The goal is to improve driver understanding, reduce avoidable escalation, and lower costs for both the public and the state.

For DMV & Public Safety Leadership

SAFE STOP CALIFORNIA is a scalable, low-friction education program designed to improve driver behavior during traffic stops while minimizing implementation burden on state agencies.

Public Safety Impact

  • Reduces escalation during routine traffic stops
  • Improves driver understanding of lawful expectations
  • Supports officer safety and communication
  • Provides consistent statewide behavioral guidance

Cost & Efficiency Benefits

  • Reduces avoidable arrests and citations
  • Lowers court and administrative workload
  • Decreases tow, impound, and detention costs
  • Minimizes implementation overhead

Implementation Advantages

  • No DMV system integration required
  • No access to DMV data or databases
  • Works within existing licensing workflows
  • Supports rapid pilot deployment

Scalable Model

  • Applies to permits, new licenses, and renewals
  • Compatible with driver education providers
  • Centralized platform enables multi-state rollout
  • Expandable to national implementation

Impact & Cost Reduction Potential

California processes millions of driver license transactions annually. Even a small reduction in escalation-related incidents can produce meaningful public safety benefits and measurable cost savings.

  • Lower court processing and enforcement costs

Demo: Program Overview

SAFE STOP is a scenario-based, real-world video training program designed to educate drivers on lawful behavior during traffic stops.

The program uses real encounter scenarios narrated by experienced law enforcement to reduce confusion, improve compliance, and prevent unnecessary escalation.

Step 1: Participant Information

The participant enters license or permit information to connect the training to the correct individual.

Information is self-reported only. No DMV verification or database access is required.

Information is self-reported and used only to associate training completion with the participant. No DMV database access is required.
